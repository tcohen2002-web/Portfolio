<div class="mira-brief">

  <p class="mira-brief__lede">We narrowed early. Of all the forms care can take, we kept circling the one closest to us — students who had moved cities or countries for university, and the parents they had left behind. Loving, intact, quietly under strain — not from neglect, but from the friction of staying close at a distance.</p>

  <p class="mira-brief__constraint"><em>"Mira had to be <span class="mira-brief__hl">an emotional mirror</span>, not a messaging app."</em> <span class="mira-brief__cite">— Self-imposed constraint</span></p>
</div>

<p class="mira-pivot"><span class="mira-pivot__eyebrow">From four streams</span>Five insights surfaced. They didn't sit on a single spectrum — they belonged to <em>two distinct sides of the same relationship</em>.</p>

<div class="mira-mirror">
  <article class="mira-mirror__side" data-side="young">
    <span class="mira-mirror__eyebrow">Young adults</span>
    <h3 class="mira-mirror__title">Need <em>room</em> — but not <em>absence</em>.</h3>
    <p>Independence from parental expectation without losing the relationship. Space to express a truer self, away from the fear of judgement. A positive family channel that doesn't ask them to perform to use it.</p>
  </article>

    <span class="mira-mirror__eyebrow">Parents</span>
    <h3 class="mira-mirror__title">Want to <em>guide</em> — and have to <em>let go</em>.</h3>
    <p>To keep guiding so their child doesn't repeat the same mistakes. To hold open communication while quietly stepping back, and to hold the worry that comes with stepping back.</p>
  </article>
</div>

<div class="mira-synthesis">
  <span class="mira-synthesis__eyebrow">The problem to design for</span>
  <p class="mira-synthesis__line">The young adult needs <em>less contact, more trust</em>. The parent needs <em>more contact, less control</em>.</p>
  <p class="mira-synthesis__coda">Any product sitting between them had to make room for both — without becoming another thing to maintain.</p>
</div>

<SectionOpener n="07" eyebrow="Part 2 · Solo refinement" title="Fifteen people. Twelve changes. One warmer Mira." />

<p class="lead">Part 1 ended with a working mid-fi. Part 2 was mine to push the rest of the way. I ran moderated tests with <strong>fifteen users</strong> — first-year students, parents, and a few fence-sitters — and grouped what they said into three patterns the concept hadn't accounted for.</p>

<div class="mira-insight" data-tone="amber">
  <div class="mira-insight__text">
    <span class="mira-insight__eyebrow">Insight i · Recognition</span>
    <blockquote class="mira-insight__quote">"I didn't realise certain elements were interactive."</blockquote>
    <p class="mira-insight__body">Soft speech bubbles and the 64% relationship-health number read as decoration. The screen looked finished — but it wasn't asking to be touched.</p>
    <p class="mira-insight__change"><span class="mira-insight__changeLabel">What changed</span>Speech bubble → labelled "Weekly check-in" button. Percentage removed entirely so attention falls on the one thing to tap.</p>
  </div>
  <figure class="mira-insight__figure">
    <Image src={iter15} alt="Before and after of the Mira home screen — left, the original mint screen with a 64% indicator and a dark speech bubble saying 'Do weekly reflection?'; right, the warmer cream-and-green redesign with a clearly labelled 'Weekly check-in' button." widths={[600, 1000, 1400]} sizes="(min-width: 900px) 640px, 92vw" loading="lazy" />
    <figcaption class="mira-insight__caption">Mid-fi → hi-fi · home screen</figcaption>
  </figure>
</div>

<div class="mira-insight" data-tone="sage">
  <div class="mira-insight__text">
    <span class="mira-insight__eyebrow">Insight ii · Navigation</span>
    <blockquote class="mira-insight__quote">"I struggle when navigating the interface and want more control."</blockquote>
    <p class="mira-insight__body">Arrow-only navigation made every screen feel like guesswork. Users wanted to know they could go back, redo, or finish — and the sliders didn't tell them where the ends meant.</p>
    <p class="mira-insight__change"><span class="mira-insight__changeLabel">What changed</span>Every screen earned labelled Back / Next / Done buttons. Sliders gained anchor labels at each interval. A redo button arrived on every voice prompt.</p>
  </div>
  <figure class="mira-insight__figure">
    <Image src={iter16} alt="Before and after of the check-in question screen — left, an early version with ambiguous arrow icons and vague slider endpoints; right, the hi-fi version with explicit 'Back' and 'Next' buttons and three anchor labels under the slider." widths={[600, 1000, 1400]} sizes="(min-width: 900px) 640px, 92vw" loading="lazy" />
    <figcaption class="mira-insight__caption">Mid-fi → hi-fi · check-in flow</figcaption>
  </figure>
</div>

<div class="mira-insight" data-tone="clay">
  <div class="mira-insight__text">
    <span class="mira-insight__eyebrow">Insight iii · Warmth</span>
    <blockquote class="mira-insight__quote">"I want it to feel warmer — not generic and flat."</blockquote>
    <p class="mira-insight__body">The mint palette was the loudest thing in the mid-fi, and users read it as clinical. Mira is meant to feel like a houseplant on a windowsill, not a hospital pamphlet — and the static <em>87%</em> wasn't carrying the emotional weight the concept asked it to.</p>
    <p class="mira-insight__change"><span class="mira-insight__changeLabel">What changed</span>Mint became cream, deep green, and warm clay. The percentage retired — the <em>whole screen</em> now takes a colour that maps to the plant's health, so the relationship's mood is felt before it's read.</p>
  </div>
  <figure class="mira-insight__figure">
    <Image src={iter25} alt="Before and after of the relationship-mood screen — left, the mid-fi shows a single mint-green smile screen with an 87% percentage and a small speech bubble; right, the hi-fi shows two screens, one warm yellow-green when the relationship is healthy and one muted sage when it needs attention." widths={[600, 1000, 1400]} sizes="(min-width: 900px) 640px, 92vw" loading="lazy" />

<div class="mira-coda">
  <span class="mira-coda__eyebrow">In closing</span>
  <h3 class="mira-coda__title">What Part 2 taught me.</h3>
  <p class="mira-coda__body">The concept was right; the expression wasn't. What read as restraint to the team that built it read as ambiguity to everyone else — a speech bubble that wasn't a button, a percentage that didn't ask to be touched, a palette that meant <em>clinical</em> to the only people who hadn't lived inside the brief. Fifteen sessions made that gap impossible to ignore. Recognition, control, and warmth are easy to talk about and harder to make true.</p>
  <p class="mira-coda__body">The other thing I'll carry forward sits on the desk. A paper flower works in mid-fi because everything else is still draft. The moment the screen earns its colour, the object has to earn its life too — or the whole thing slips back into prop. Hi-fi isn't a polish pass. It's the round where the design either commits to being the thing it claims to be, or quietly admits it isn't.</p>
</div>
