<SectionOpener n="01" eyebrow="A tribute" title="Three years in Mallorca. One small box." />

<div class="vamos-opener">
  <p class="vamos-opener__lede">I trained at the <span class="vamos-opener__hl">Rafa Nadal Academy in Mallorca</span> for three years — long enough to know the heat of the clay, the rhythm of warm-ups before sunrise, and the small rituals players keep to themselves. When DECO2018 asked for a giftable object built across three fabrication methods, I didn't reach for a brief. I reached for a person.</p>

  <p class="vamos-opener__motto"><em>Smash cravings, not your racquet.</em></p>

<SectionOpener n="03" eyebrow="The first idea" title="A bull that didn't make it to chocolate." />

<div class="vamos-pivot">
  <p class="vamos-pivot__lede">The first sketch was a bull's head. It checked every box on paper — Spain in one stroke, and a quiet wink to Rafa's own silhouette logo, the charging bull. I went into tutor consults proud of it.</p>

  <div class="vamos-pivot__body">
    <p>The tutors were kind but direct: a bull head, in chocolate, at this scale, with these printable mould draft angles? The horns alone were going to fight me — undercuts, demoulding, finish quality. By the third consult it was clear the geometry wasn't going to survive contact with a vacuum-formed mould.</p>

    <blockquote class="vamos-pivot__pull">
      <p>The ambition didn't disappear.<br/>It migrated.</p>
    </blockquote>

    <p>So I let the bull go. The chocolates stayed honest — a racquet and two balls, forms that <em>want</em> to be moulded. Everything I'd wanted the bull to carry — the Spanishness, the ritual, the sense of <em>occasion</em> — got rebuilt into the box.</p>
  </div>

</div>

<SectionOpener n="06" eyebrow="M·C — The render" title="Creating the materials." />

<div class="vamos-render">
  <div class="vamos-render__body">
    <p><span class="vamos-render__kicker">Palette</span>The material-showcase brief made me consider what the surfaces should <em>mean</em> as well as how they look. Gold and Spain's red and yellow led the palette: gold to honour a champion and his Olympic history; red and yellow to declare Spanish identity, echo clay, and nod to the tennis ball.</p>

    <p><span class="vamos-render__kicker">Trial</span>I trialled plastic and cardboard outers — both felt everyday. A full-wood shell had warmth, but softened Rafa's attitude. That pivoted me to refine the form and weave in recognisable cues: Spanish multi-coloured bull eyes for quiet pride, and small gold Nike ticks like jewellery, connecting the object to his lifelong sponsor.</p>

    <p><span class="vamos-render__kicker">Staging</span>I re-staged the opening so the horns read like lightning as the lid lifts, framing the chocolate racquet and two balls. Across passes I sharpened planes and tuned materials until it balanced restraint with energy. Six materials, one frame.</p>
